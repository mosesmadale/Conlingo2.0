The victory of light over darkness represents the triumph of truth, knowledge, and goodness over falsehood, ignorance, and evil. Similarly, Christmas is often seen as a festival of light in many parts of India. It's associated with the birth of Jesus Christ, who is considered the light of the world. Both festivals signify the triumph of light over darkness, emphasizing hope, peace, and redemption. These common themes bring people together across different cultures and faiths, fostering unity and shared values. By embracing our diverse traditions, we can appreciate the beauty of these commonalities and deepen our understanding of one another. (approx. 170 words)

15. How does food during Christmas vary regionally across India?

There's also an array of sweet treats like payasam, halwa, and fruit salads. On the other hand, in cities like Bangalore or Hyderabad, you may find more Westernized influences in their Christmas celebrations, including cakes, cookies, and even turkey!
